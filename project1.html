Japan

Japan is a land of stark contrasts. In the summer months, the sun doesn't set at all in the northernmost parts of the country. In the winter, the opposite happens, and the sun disappears for months. The white snow, bright moon and stars, and - if you're lucky - the colourful Northern Lights, create a surprisingly light and magical nightscape. It has been recognized as the happiest country in the world for several years running.

Things to do in Japan

Himeji Castle is the largest and most visited castle in Japan, and it was registered in 1993 as one of the first UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the country.
